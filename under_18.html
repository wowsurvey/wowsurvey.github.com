MMORPG Study: Parental Permission / Informed Consent

Please have your parent or legal guardian read this form with you and give permission for your participation in this research study.

The following information is provided to inform you about the research project and your child’s participation in it. Please read this form carefully and any questions you may have about this study and the information given below.

Your child’s participation in this research study is voluntary. Your child is also free to withdraw from this study at any time. In the event new information becomes available that may affect the risks or benefits associated with this research study or your child’s willingness to participate in it, you will be notified so that you can make an informed decision whether or not to continue your child’s participation in this study.

For additional information about giving consent or your rights as a participant in this study, please feel free to contact Emily Born at the Middle Tennessee State University Office of Compliance at (615) 494-8918 or complain@mtsu.edu.

DESCRIPTION:

I understand that my child will be asked to participate in an online survey research study on the social interaction patterns and preferences of MMORPG players. If your child is currently a resident of the United States of America and plays one or more MMORPGs, your child is eligible to participate. Your child will be asked to complete a 5-10 minute survey. Your child will not be paid or reimbursed for their participation.

RISKS AND BENEFITS:

The risks associated with this survey study are minimal, and are comparable to the risks involved with casual web browsing. The benefits that may reasonably be expected to result from this study are empirical findings that would enhance the understanding of the social interaction patterns and preferences of MMORPG players.

TIME INVOLVEMENT:

Your participation in this study will take approximately 5-10 minutes.

CONFIDENTIALITY:

All efforts, within reason, will be made to keep the personal information in your research record private, but total privacy cannot be promised. Your information may be shared with Middle Tennessee State University or the government, such as MTSU Institutional Review Board and the Federal Government Office for Human Research Protections.

SUBJECT'S RIGHTS:

I understand that my child's participation is voluntary and that he/she has the right to withdraw their consent or discontinue participation at any time without penalty. I understand that my child has the right to refuse to answer particular questions. I also understand that his/her individual privacy will be maintained in all published and written data resulting from the study.
